Correlations between Health Risks, Income and Age

    This visualization provides meaningful insights about health risks, income and age, which vary by state. As shown in the chart, there are some notable observations:
    > Texas has the highest % of its citizens lacking healthcare at 24.9%, despite having a median income of $53,035 (average income is ~$54,454). Further research would be needed to determine the contributing factors.
    > The highest obesity rate is concentrated between age group 34-40. Arkansas has the highest rate at 35.9% and average age at 38.
    > The lower the median household income, the higher the percentage rate of smokers.
